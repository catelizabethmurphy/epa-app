Drinking-water testing

1,716 U.S. water systems tested above the EPA standards Biden set for drinking water.

Of 10,166 public water systems in EPA's national PFAS sampling, 17% reported at least one sample above the federal limit for one of the five PFAS the Biden EPA finalized standards for in 2024 — PFOA, PFOS, PFHxS, PFNA, and HFPO-DA (GenX). The Trump EPA is now moving to rescind those standards for every PFAS except PFOA and PFOS.

Source: EPA Fifth Unregulated Contaminant Monitoring Rule (UCMR 5) public water system results. Sampling window 2023-01-03 through 2025-12-30.

What's your state doing about it?

As the Trump EPA rolls back the federal drinking-water standards, states are filling the gap — restricting PFAS in consumer products, food packaging, firefighting foam, and industrial discharges.

See the state legislation tracker →

The five PFAS maximum contaminant levels — 4 parts per trillion for PFOA and PFOS, 10 ppt for PFHxS, PFNA, and HFPO-DA (GenX) — were finalized by the Biden EPA in April 2024 as part of the National Primary Drinking Water Regulation. In May 2025 the Trump EPA announced it would keep the PFOA and PFOS standards but rescind the limits for the other three compounds, and proposed extending compliance deadlines to 2031. "Over limit" means a system reported at least one sample above its compound's EPA standard.
