Nevada drinking-water testing

4 of 58 Nevada water systems reported PFAS above the EPA standards Biden set in 2024.

7% of public water systems in Nevada that EPA sampled for PFAS reported at least one result above the standards Biden's EPA finalized in 2024 for PFOA, PFOS, PFHxS, PFNA, or HFPO-DA (GenX). The Trump EPA is now moving to rescind those standards for every PFAS except PFOA and PFOS.

What is Nevada doing about PFAS?

As the federal drinking-water standards retreat, states are filling the gap with their own restrictions on PFAS in consumer products, food packaging, firefighting foam, and industrial discharges.

Source: EPA Fifth Unregulated Contaminant Monitoring Rule (UCMR 5). "Worst exceedance" shows how many times above the EPA standard the system's highest sample registered, and which compound it was. Biden's 2024 rule set limits of 4 ppt for PFOA and PFOS and 10 ppt for PFHxS, PFNA, and HFPO-DA (GenX). The Trump EPA proposed rescinding the latter three in May 2025.
