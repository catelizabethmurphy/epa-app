Trump I
2017–2021

Recognizing the problem

The first Trump administration was the first to formally acknowledge PFAS — a class of synthetic compounds known as "forever chemicals" — as a national crisis. The Trump EPA proposed a number of actions, but stopped short of enacting binding rules.

{# ══ CHAPTER: BIDEN ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ #}
Biden
2021–2025

Setting standards

Under the Biden administration, the EPA for the first time set enforceable limits on PFAS levels in drinking water and designated the chemicals as hazardous substances.

{# ══ CHAPTER: TRUMP II ═════════════════════════════════════════════════════ #}
Trump II
2025–present

Reversing course ... some of the time

Under the second Trump administration, the EPA's PFAS record has been inconsistent: It's taken action to rescind four of the six PFAS limits defined under Biden — while also announcing its intention to uphold the hazardous substance designation.
