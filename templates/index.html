Federal Policy Record Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances · 2017–present

PFAS are forever. The Trump administration's stance on them is not.

Tracking what three administrations promised — and did — on PFAS regulation. Data from the Federal Register, EPA's newsroom, and regulations.gov.

{# ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════ INTRO ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════ #}

Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances — a class of roughly 12,000 synthetic chemicals found in the blood of 97 percent of Americans — have been the subject of a decade-long federal regulatory effort that is now being unwound.

Under the Biden administration, EPA finalized the first enforceable drinking water limits for PFAS and designated two compounds as Superfund hazardous substances. In May 2025, the Trump EPA rescinded four of those six drinking water standards. Two weeks later, the White House's own chronic-disease commission named PFAS a driver of childhood illness. This tracker documents that record.
